Ideation Phase – Economic Freedom Analysis Project Project Overview

This phase focuses on understanding user needs, defining problem statements, and generating innovative ideas for the project “Measuring the Pulse of Prosperity: An Index of Economic Freedom Analysis.”
The ideation process includes brainstorming, problem definition, and empathy mapping to ensure solutions are user-centered and practical.

2. Define Problem Statements:

This phase identifies customer challenges and clarifies user goals.

Purpose

Understand customer perspective

Identify barriers faced by users

Define emotional and practical challenges

Example Problem Statements

PS-1: Policymaker

Goal: Understand drivers of economic prosperity

Issue: Existing indices are complex and lack timely updates

Need: A comprehensive and regularly updated index

Emotion: Frustrated and uncertain about policy choices

PS-2: Economic Researcher

Goal: Identify correlations between economic freedom and outcomes

Issue: Fragmented and inconsistent datasets

Need: Standardized and accessible data

Emotion: Overwhelmed by analysis challenges

3. Empathy Map Canvas

The empathy mapping stage helps teams understand user behaviors, thoughts, and feelings.

Purpose:

Capture user attitudes and motivations

Understand pain points and expected gains

Improve solution design through user perspective

Empathy Map Components

Think & Feel – worries, aspirations, priorities

Hear – influence from peers and environment

See – market and external factors

Say & Do – actions and behaviors

Pain – frustrations and obstacles

Gain – goals and success measures
